Welcome

I am a PhD student in Economics at the University of Glasgow. My research interests include labour economics, education economics, applied econometrics, and causal inference.

My current research examines the effects of taking a gap year before higher education on early-career labour market outcomes.

Work in progres

Exact Parametric Causal Mediation Analysis with a Categorical Mediator with Santiago Montoya-Blandon
This paper develops a parametric framework for causal mediation analysis with nominal categorical mediators, such as occupation, education, or sector. Building on Imai et al. (2010), I derive closed-form expressions for the direct and indirect effects on the natural outcome scale, with the indirect effect decomposed additively by mediator category. The paper also clarifies the relationship with inverse odds ratio weighting, highlighting its strengths and limitations. Simulations show that the proposed approach performs well, especially when treatment-mediator interactions are present.
The Cost of a Gap Year: Education Delay and Career Outcomes with Anwen Zhang and Santiago Montoya-Blandon
This paper examines the association between taking a gap year before higher education and early-career earnings in the UK. Using the Next Steps cohort linked to administrative school records, I compare students who defer university entry with those who enrol immediately, using OLS, matching, and sensitivity analyses. Gap-year participation is associated with lower earnings at ages 25 and 32, though the penalty declines over time. The effects are largest for unplanned and non-labour-market gap years, while labour-market-oriented gap years show little effect. The findings highlight the importance of gap-year type for long-term outcomes.
